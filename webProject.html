StArt Website

A website redesign for Harvard's Studio Art Society.

OVERVIEW

A website redesign for Harvard Studio Art Society. An personal project made to improve the visual identity, navigation, and responsiveness of the site, and draw more members to the organization.

CONTRIBUTIONS
I was the principal UX/UI designer and developer. This was an independent project I overtook to improve the visibility of Studio Art Society online and on campus.

MOTIVATION

As a member of the executive board of Studio Art Society, the move to virtual programming during Fall 2020 required us to adapt to a new online format and to think of new ways to grow and sustain our membership. Our previous website was both outdated and did not best reflect a serious arts organization, so I sought to change that. From this, the StArt website redesign was born.

Outlined is a page by page deconstruct of the previous StArt website, which gives five key problems that will guide the redesign and open up new pathways for growth.

1. Visual Identity
The StArt website lacks visual cohesion between pages in typography, spacing, sizing, and color scheme.

2. Outdated information
The sections meant to inform are not properly updated and do not contain navigation to StArt's other social media accounts.

3. Difficult navigation
The website is one long page, with a difficult to see navigation bar, making it hard for users to move between sections without scrolling.

4. Information Hierarchy
Section content is not organized in an intuitive way.

5. Responsiveness
The website is not mobile-friendly, which ignores one of the main ways college students will access the site.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Creating a plan and developing a visual identity.

A site map made to outline content and page connectivity. The current site is designed specifically to respond to each pain point, showcase member art, and draw in new membership.

THE SOLUTION

Creating the new and improved StArt site.

A style guide for typography and color scheme. Using this guide and the new information architecture I arrived that the website's complete redesign.

1 Bold landing page that showcases member art.

Users are immediately presented with art pieces by current members and led to news about the organization. Also included as a "where to find us" section, something crucial as campus begins to re-densify and operations will exist both in person and online.

2 Club info and mission all in one place.

The previously separated "About Us" and "Meet the Team" sections are combined into one main "About" page, adhering to an information hierarchy in order to most effectively display content about StArt.

3 Cross-platform connectivity, and straight-to-the-point text and navigation.

All pages are immediately accessible from each other. The navigation bar is also prominent and contains links to all relevant social media platforms. Text contains only the most succient and updated information with important text highlighted.

4 Responsive platform with visual cohesion.

A website made for responsiveness across all screen sizes and that adheres to the style guide to create a cohesive visual identity for StArt. A black and white color scheme is used to make the student artwork and highlighted text visually prominent.

The mobile UI and its strengths: A platform made for college students.

The StArt website's mobile design is made by and for busy college students. A group often looking to get the most information, the fastest way possible. All screens are is at most two clicks away from each other. An interactive map, powered by Bing is included so students can navigate to the club's location on campus.
